What do the metrics mean?

All three consistency metrics are non-negative costs. Lower is better: 0 means that flavor of consistency is preserved exactly. Larger values mean more drift, summed only over elements persisting across the two frames.

  • positional — Σ‖D(n) − D′(n)‖² over persisting nodes. Strict; "every node held still." Penlloy PLATEAU 2025 §6.2.
  • relative — Σ‖(D(n₂) − D(n₁)) − (D′(n₂) − D′(n₁))‖² over persisting edges. "Every arrow kept its direction and length"; the whole diagram could shift as a unit and this stays 0. Penlloy §6.2.
  • pairwise distance — Σ(d_curr(i,j) − d_prev(i,j))² over every pair of persisting nodes. "The shape of the node cloud was preserved"; invariant under translation AND rotation. Computational form of Liang TOSEM 2026 §3.4 partial consistency.

constraint adherence is different — it is a fairness score on the solver, not a consistency cost on the policy. Higher is better. It reports the fraction of the layout spec's Left/Top/Alignment constraints satisfied at the post-solver positions. In healthy runs it stays at 1.0 across every policy, even randomPositioning; that flatness is the visible proof that the partial orders the user declared are honored regardless of seed strategy.
